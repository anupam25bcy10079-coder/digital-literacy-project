                                             UPI FRAUD (CASE STUDY)

UPI/Online payment fraud is a common cybercrime where people are tricked into sending money through fake links or payment requests. A common example is when a person receives a call or message from someone pretending to be a bank employee or a buyer from an online platform. The fraudster asks the victim to scan a QR code or click on a payment link, claiming that money will be received. However, instead of receiving money, the victim ends up paying the fraudster.

This usually happens step-by-step. First, the fraudster gains trust by acting professionally. Then they create panic, like telling that the offer will expire soon. After that, they send a fake payment request or QR code. Once the victim enters their UPI PIN, the money gets deducted instantly.

Students and people who are new to digital payments are often targeted because they are not fully aware of such scams. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and sometimes loss of trust in online systems. In some cases, victims are unable to recover their money.

This type of fraud shows how important it is to stay alert while using digital payment apps.
